## Why this worked

The idea was simple: Bangkok pushed eighty years forward, but still humid, crowded, neon, and human.

Most generated city footage becomes generic because the prompt asks for a vibe instead of constraints. I wrote a short style bible first.

## Style rules

- Wet roads and reflected signage.
- Food stalls next to impossible architecture.
- Human scale in every establishing shot.
- No anonymous cyberpunk skyline unless there is a street-level detail anchoring it.

## Production note

The best shots came from prompts that described a camera position, a time of day, and one specific contradiction. "A temple market under a floating rail interchange" worked better than "futuristic Bangkok."

Specificity made the city feel less synthetic.
